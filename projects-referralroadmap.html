Transforming the Mental Health Referral System through AI

03.2029 – 05.2029 · @TU Delft · Master Thesis

To address mental health referral system challenges, including healthcare professionals' workload burden and waiting delays in the system, I developed a future strategy (strategic roadmap & tactical roadmap) of AI integration to improve the mental health referral system.

Background

The Dutch mental healthcare system faces prolonged waiting lists and fragmented handovers between GPs and GGZ. Through literature review and stakeholder conversations, I mapped systemic bottlenecks (information loss, eligibility ambiguity, and delayed feedback). These informed principles for responsible AI adoption across referral touchpoints.

System context map highlighting bottlenecks across GP–GGZ referral.

Process / Methods

Mixed-methods design research combined desk research, expert interviews, journey/system mapping, and co-creation sessions. Insights were synthesized into a system-level framework and validated via critique sessions with mental healthcare supporters.

Methods and synthesis: interviews, mapping, and framework consolidation.

Outcome

The final deliverable is a two-layer roadmap: (1) a strategic roadmap outlining horizon shifts (H1–H3) for the referral ecosystem, and (2) a tactical roadmap detailing AI-supported actions (triage assistance, interoperable handovers, and monitoring feedback loops). The roadmaps align with EU regulation themes (transparency, privacy, safety & efficacy, interoperability, risk management, and user rights).
