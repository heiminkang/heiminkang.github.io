Transforming the Mental Health Referral System through AI

03.2025 – 08.2025 @TU Delft · Master Thesis

To address mental health referral system challenges, including healthcare professionals' workload burden and waiting delays in the system, I developed a future strategy (strategic roadmap & tactical roadmap) of AI integration to improve the mental health referral system.

Background

Long waits and fragmented handovers slow down GP → GGZ referrals.

The Dutch mental healthcare system faces prolonged waiting lists and fragmented handovers between GPs and GGZ. Through literature review and stakeholder conversations, I mapped systemic bottlenecks—information loss, eligibility ambiguity, and delayed feedback—which informed principles for responsible AI adoption across referral touchpoints.

Methods

Mixed-methods research to build a system-level picture.

Desk research, expert interviews, journey & system mapping, and co-creation sessions. Insights were synthesized into a system framework and validated via critique sessions with mental healthcare supporters.
